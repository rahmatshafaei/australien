Uluru

Uluru or Ayers Rock as it is also called, is Australia's most famous natural landmark.Uluru is most known as Ayers Rock and it was named by William Gosse in 1873 after Sir Henry Ayers. Uluru is the officiel Aboriginal name of the mountain. The mountain was created for about 600 million years ago and the Aborigines have lived there for over 10000 years. Ayers Rock was originally located at the bottom of the sea, but today the mountain stands 863 meter above sea level and 348 meters above the ground. The Ayers Rock is longer than several famous buildings around the world including the Eiffel tower in Paris and Chrysler building in New York.
