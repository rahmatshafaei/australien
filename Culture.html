Culture

Australia is an incredibly diverse country and is home to so many different wonderful cultures and communities. Australian culture is primarily a Western culture, derived from the United Kingdom. Drinking and playing are important aspects for the people of australia.Owing to a vibrant and multiculturalism, because of that Australia doesn’t really share one cohesive cuisine and the Australians are eating their national symbol kangaroo.



Australia has an exciting calendar of events and festivals, where locals and tourists alike gather to celebrate food, sports, art and culture. Australia hosts a huge number of world's renowned sporting events like the australian open tennis tournament and Sydney to Hobart yacht race. When it comes to food, the western australia gourmet escape is a highlight.



For Australians, a stranger is a mateship that you haven't met yet. Mateship is an Australian culture idiom that incarent equality, loyalty and friendship. Migrants and outlanders are often amazed at how openly and fast Australians begin to create this mateship. Relationships are built on friendship then hierarchy. In this way, people tend to show respect for friends and peers through the motion of equality instead of respect. It’s considered bad to ask someone to do anything you would not do yourself. People who value themselves higher than their mates are quickly reminded of their place. Australians pride themselves on being good friends and neighbors, not just to people they know. Australian people tend to greet everyone from postman to taxi driver.



According to UN reports , Australia is one of the most prosperous countries in the world. “The lucky country” has become the nickname for Australia and it is the right name for a country that has such a happy climate and is politically stable.
